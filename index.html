Building My Practice as an Adult Educator

A reflective journey through the Master of Adult Education (MAdEd) program at Saint Francis Xavier University (StFX).

Welcome

This portfolio presents my growth as an adult educator through reflection, evidence, and critical analysis. It is organized using a house-building metaphor to illustrate the ongoing development of my practice.

Each section represents a critical phase of constructing a house, from the underlying philosophical foundation to the final structural framework and the evidence of the work within.

Land Acknowledgment

I would like to acknowledge that the land on which we gather is the ancestral and unceded territory of the Indigenous Peoples of Newfoundland and Labrador. This includes the Mi'kmaq, Innu, Inuit, and Southern Inuit. The island of Newfoundland is part of Mi'kma'ki, the ancestral and unceded territory of the Mi'kmaq People, and we also recognize the island as the homeland of the Beothuk People, whose culture and way of life were tragically lost to colonial violence.

In Labrador, we recognize the traditional lands of the Innu Nation and the Inuit, including the Southern Inuit of NunatuKavut (noo-nah-TOO-kah-voot). These lands have been home to Indigenous Peoples for millennia, and their deep connections to the land, water, and communities continue to shape this region.

We honour the histories, cultures, and traditions of all Indigenous Peoples in Newfoundland and Labrador, and we acknowledge their enduring contributions to the richness of this place. As settlers, we commit to building relationships based on respect, reciprocity, and reconciliation as we move forward together.

My Focus

My portfolio represents my development as an adult educator who supports adult learners in navigating transitions by making technology education accessible, practical, and meaningful, with a focus on applied, career-oriented learning that leads to personal and professional growth.

This image is one of the Newfoundland & Labrador flag I took in the Quidi Vidi village in St. John's, NL. It represents the importance of place and context in shaping my perspective as an educator. Just as the flag symbolizes the unique identity of Newfoundland & Labrador, my experiences as a Newfoundlander have deeply influenced how I understand learners and design educational experiences that are relevant and meaningful to their lives. I took this picture as part of my therapy journey after my PTSD diagnosis.
