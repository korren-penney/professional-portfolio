Design & Technology in Practice
Hands-On Digital Learning

Beyond the Browser Podcast

This multimedia artifact created for ADED545: Critical Review of Literature in Adult Education explores the intersection of critical pedagogy and digital literacy through a conversational lens. Moving "Beyond the Browser," I examine how my professional work with TechPrep serves as a laboratory for applying the transformative theories of Paulo Freire and bell hooks to the digital divide.


Drawing on critical pedagogy, particularly the work of Freire (2017) and hooks (1994), this podcast examines how digital learning environments can either reinforce passive forms of learning or support the development of critical awareness and agency. Farag et al. (2021) extend this thinking into digital contexts, highlighting how access alone is not enough if learners are not supported in developing confidence and voice.


The podcast centers on a fundamental question: Is digital education a tool for liberation or a modern site of banking-model oppression? Using the story of a learner named Dani, I trace the journey from "digital oppression" (toxic workplace environments and self-doubt) to "digital agency" (finding joy and confidence through learning).

Accessibility & Inclusion
Inclusive Practice

Designing for Access and Confidence

Accessibility in my practice is not treated as an additional feature, but as a core design consideration. In technology-based learning environments, learners enter with varying levels of experience, confidence, and access, which requires intentional planning to reduce barriers. In my teaching, this includes providing multiple ways to engage with content, offering structured guidance alongside opportunities for independent exploration, and creating space for learners to build confidence before being expected to perform independently.


This approach reflects a social justice perspective on digital literacy, recognizing that access to tools alone is not sufficient without the support needed to use them effectively and confidently (Smythe, 2022).
