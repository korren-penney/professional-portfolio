Framework

How my teaching practice is structured and put into action.

My Teaching Framework

This section outlines the framework that guides my teaching practice. It represents how I move from my beliefs about learning into the decisions I make when designing and facilitating learning experiences.

Approach to Learning

My approach is grounded in applied, learner-centered education. I prioritize hands-on, problem-based learning that allows learners to actively engage with content and develop skills they can use beyond the classroom.

Design Strategies

I design learning experiences that connect to real-world contexts and challenges. This ensures that learning is relevant and supports learners in applying their knowledge in practical ways.

Learner Support

I create structured, supportive environments where learners can progress with confidence. This includes clear expectations, consistent guidance, and responsiveness to individual needs.

Technology in Practice

Technology is used as a tool to support learning, not as an end goal. I focus on helping learners build both technical skills and the confidence to apply them in meaningful ways.

Accessibility & Inclusion

Accessibility and inclusion are embedded throughout my practice. I design with an awareness of how instructional choices can either create barriers or open pathways for participation.

In Practice

This framework reflects how I support adult learners in building skills that are directly connected to their goals, while creating learning environments that are structured, inclusive, and responsive.

From Practice to Philosophy

The approaches and strategies in this framework reflect how I currently teach. However, they are grounded in a deeper set of beliefs about learning, experience, and the role of the educator. These beliefs are explored more fully in my philosophy.
